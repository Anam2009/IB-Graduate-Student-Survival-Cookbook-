About This Cookbook

As part of our CAS project, we created The IB Graduate Student Survival Cookbook — a practical and student-friendly resource designed to support IB graduates as they transition to university life. The cookbook is built for students who may be new to cooking and need simple, affordable, and time-efficient meals that fit around demanding workloads and the realities of independent living.

Throughout the project, we collaborated to research, test, and refine 12–20 beginner-friendly recipes spanning breakfasts, lunches, dinners, and quick meals. Our work involved developing recipes, writing clear step-by-step instructions, taking original photographs, and designing the final layout — applying original thinking to recipe development, visual design, and presentation to create something genuinely meaningful and practical.

This cookbook supports student well-being by promoting healthy, affordable eating and building essential life skills: budgeting, time management, and basic cooking — challenges faced by students all around the world. Ethical considerations were central to our process; we ensured all recipes are safe, low-cost, beginner-friendly, and inclusive of different dietary needs.

This website is our gift to Year 12 IB graduates as part of their farewell from the IB programme, directly supporting them as they begin university life.

Our Goals

  • To creatively design and contribute to a student-friendly cookbook through recipe development, testing, and layout design.
  • To support student well-being by providing practical guidance on affordable, healthy eating for IB graduates at university.
  • To help students develop independence and essential life skills such as cooking, budgeting, and time management.
  • To make ethical and globally aware choices by creating inclusive, safe, and accessible recipes relevant to students worldwide.

We hope this cookbook makes your university cooking journey a little easier — and a lot more enjoyable. Enjoy cooking!
